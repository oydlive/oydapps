Two-Stream Model: Concert vs. Rental
RENTAL SHOWS (4 events)
~$49K
Revenue in the door. Near-zero talent cost. Fixed overhead still applies.
CONCERT SHOWS (4 events)
High risk
Ticket revenue is primary, but sponsorship is the difference between loss and profit. Night of Nicks was losing money at near-sellout.
Lean recommendation: Treat these as two distinct value streams with separate KPIs. For rentals, the VSM should focus on rental agreement standardization and F&B maximization. For concerts, the VSM should focus on sponsor pipeline velocity and advance sales. They share infrastructure but they're different businesses.
01
● High Priority
Get Direct F&B POS Access
F&B is your second-largest revenue line and the data lives behind Pat. Every settlement is delayed or estimated because you can't pull numbers yourself. This single access change would eliminate 2–3 days of settlement wait time on every show.
  • Request login credentials from Marc / Pat for POS system
  • Build a post-show F&B report template in Prism
  • Set F&B number pull as a Day+1 standard task in ClickUp
  • Add F&B close-out to security/staffing briefing checklist
Eliminates: 2–3 day settlement delay per show. Removes dependency on Pat and Kylene for core financial data.
02
● High Priority
Activate the 15K Buyer List
You have 15,000+ ticket buyer emails sitting in Ticketmaster from years of LECOM events. This is your highest-value marketing asset and it's dormant. Every paid Facebook ad you run is an admission that you can't reach warm buyers. Fix this before the next show.
  • Set up Hive.co (already demoed) with the exported CSV
  • Create events@ LECOM send domain — Kylene or Marc to authorize
  • Segment by genre / past event type for targeted sends
  • Build automated post-show "thanks + next show" sequence
  • Kill cold Facebook ad spend — shift to list-first marketing
Eliminates: Cold ad waste. Reduces customer acquisition cost from ~$X/ticket to near-zero for warm buyers.
03
● High Priority
Standardize Settlement in Prism
Settlement is being run differently each show — different versions sent, uncertainty about which is authoritative, facility fees accidentally added, TM numbers needing double-verification. You need one settlement template, one approval chain, and one filing location.
  • Build master Prism settlement template (concert + rental versions)
  • Define: Internal settlement ≠ External settlement — Jason reviews external before send
  • Create W-9 collection checklist — collect at contract signing, not post-show
  • Add "TM Double Tap" reminder as standard Day+7 task
  • Establish 48h settlement target from show close
Eliminates: Rework, confusion, late artist payments. Builds trust with venue finance (Jodi/Deane).
04
● High Priority
Break Kylene Dependency — Direct Dept Access
Kylene is the single point of failure for staffing, security invoices, F&B numbers, social media, comp approvals, and changeover coordination. This is identified in the chat logs and confirmed in the March 3rd org chart conversation. Marc has authorized going direct to department leads.
  • Map all recurring Kylene touchpoints to their direct department owners
  • Get direct contacts: Pat (F&B), security vendor, changeover crew
  • Send Marc the org chart with OYD's recommended communication flow
  • CC Kylene on communications initially — fade over 60 days
Eliminates: The most cited bottleneck in 3 months of chat logs. Reduces average task wait time by estimated 2–5 days per show.
05
● Medium Priority
Sponsorship Pipeline SOP
27 sponsorship tasks are overdue. The root cause isn't lack of leads — it's lack of a defined follow-up process. Five Star Bank is stuck because local reps can't authorize. M&T Bank needs a warm intro from Marc. Each one has a different next step that isn't documented.
  • Define 3-stage escalation: Email → Call → Marc intro → Park for 90d
  • Set 7-day follow-up SLA as ClickUp automation
  • Use Marc's network explicitly — identify who in his rolodex can unblock each deal
  • Create tiered sponsorship packages ($2.5K / $5K / $10K / Title) with clear deliverables
  • Weekly 15-min sponsorship pipeline review
Target: Close 3 of 5 top stalled sponsors by next show. At $2.5–5K each = $7.5–15K that can make a marginal concert profitable.
06
● Medium Priority
Front-Load Artist Logistics
W-9 collection, production invoices, ASCAP licensing, and advance sheets are all being chased after booking instead of integrated into the contracting workflow. This creates show-week scrambles that are visible in the chat logs for every concert.
  • Contract checklist: W-9, payment info, insurance cert, tech rider required at signing
  • Advance sheet template in Google Drive — assigned Day+14 after contract
  • Production invoice: required 30 days before show, not day-of
  • ASCAP: confirm annual license covers all shows — one action, not per-show
Eliminates: Show-week fire drills. Reduces Mike's coordination load the week of each show by estimated 30–40%.
07
● Next Phase
Separate Social Channels — LECOM Events vs. Hockey
Concert marketing is competing with hockey content on the same feeds. Chris and Jason have identified this and Marc has been notified. A dedicated LECOM Events page would allow event-specific targeting, algorithm optimization, and audience growth without hockey diluting it.
  • Present Covelli Center / Youngstown Phantoms example to Marc as model
  • Create LECOM Events Center Instagram and Facebook pages
  • Migrate event content — keep existing LECOM page for hockey/hockey fans
  • Add "follow us" CTA to every show for next-event notification
Builds a dedicated concert audience over time — reduces dependence on paid ads and cold outreach per show.
08
● Next Phase
Rental Agreement Standardization
Rental shows are low-risk but the contracting is ad hoc. No minimum rental ($5K discussed for NCAA but not formalized). Revenue share triggers not standard. Jason created an OSFL Orchestra template in Feb — that's the right direction. Now productize it.
  • Prism rental template: min $9K covering security + staffing + changeover + cleaning
  • Rev share clause: only triggers once fixed expenses recouped

  • F&B mandatory for all rental events — don't leave it as opt-in
Turns rental shows from "low-risk break-even" to "reliable profitable base." Funds concert risk-taking.
